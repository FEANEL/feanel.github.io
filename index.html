Introduction

Large Language Models (LLMs) have unlocked unprecedented potential for personalised education, yet their fine-grained feedback on K-12 English writing remains largely unexplored. Existing systems usually provide (1) surface-level grammatical fixes or (2) coarse holistic scores, leaving teachers and students without actionable or error-specific guidance.

We introduce FEANEL‚ÄîFine-grained Error ANalysis for English Learners‚Äîa new benchmark expressly designed to evaluate and advance the pedagogical abilities of LLMs. FEANEL contains 1,000 student essays (Grades 3-12) with 8,676 expert-annotated error analyses built upon a 29-category, part-of-speech-driven taxonomy. All annotations include Error Type, Severity (1-5), and a human-written explanation. The main advantages of FEANEL over previous datasets are summarized as follows:

1. Pedagogical Granularity. Each edit is labelled with type + severity + explanation, enabling models to go far beyond binary ‚Äúcorrect/incorrect‚Äù judgements.
2. Expert-Curated Taxonomy. A mutually-exclusive, 29-class hierarchy co-designed with veteran English educators eliminates the ambiguity of earlier ad-hoc tags.
3. Real K-12 Coverage. Balanced sampling of 500 elementary and 500 secondary essays captures diverse proficiency levels and authentic school-specific error patterns.
4. Rigorous Quality Control. Dual-phase annotation, cross-validation, and manual explanation selection ensure high fidelity; no synthetic errors or model-generated labels are used.
5. Comprehensive Evaluation Suite. We assess LLMs across Accuracy, Macro-F1, MAE, and text-generation metrics (BLEU, METEOR, ROUGE-L), offering a 360¬∞ view of linguistic and pedagogical competence.

Benchmarking state-of-the-art LLMs on FEANEL reveals significant gaps: even the best models mis-classify complex edits and miss the subtle instructional tone found in human feedback. Performance varies markedly with prompt design and task ordering, underscoring the need for specialised methods.

By providing a high-quality dataset, a unified error taxonomy for English writing, and a thorough evaluation protocol, FEANEL paves the way for next-generation educational LLMs that deliver precise, interpretable, and learner-centric feedback.
